projects

I worked as a Research Assistant at the Guilford College Cline Observatory in the summer of 2018, studying variable stars and constructing a system for automatic data analysis and collection. The project was intended primarily to facilitate future student research using the tools I developed. While the nature of the project did not necessitate a final research paper or presentation, I am compiling a summary of the work I did and the preliminary results obtained. That document will be available here soon.

For my final project for an observational astronomy class at Guilford College, I used a method called Lucky Imaging to photograph planets and incoorporate them into artistic photographs. The project was exhibited as a poster presentation at the Guilford Undergraduate Symposium, and turned into an article which was published in the October 2018 edition of The Physics Teacher.

While not a full-fledged research project, I have used Geographic Information Systems (GIS) software to study the impacts of urban sprawl on developing cities. This was a final project for a GIS class at Guilford College, completed over three weeks in the fall of 2018. The final paper can be found here.

In the Spring of 2018, I worked with a team of fellow students at the Early College at Guilford to complete the SIAM Mathworks Math Modelling Challenge, a 14-hour long math competition. We developed a model to address food waste in the U.S. and submitted a paper which won Honerable Mention in the competition. The final paper can be seen here.
