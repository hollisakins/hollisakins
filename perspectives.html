projects // photography // perspectives

These images are part of an ongoing series exporing the technique of combination printing: a darkroom process that involves printing several images on one page. The technique was pioneered by Jerry Uelsmann, and I embraced it as a way to enjoy the photographic process regardless of its efficiency. Here is a blog post from the spring of 2017 detailing the printing technique. Each of these images involves in some way the relationship between the sky and the ground, the otherwordly and the worldly.

All of the images here are digital scans of 11x14 or 8x10 silver gelatin prints, typically made from several enlargers projecting images from 120mm film. All film and paper were developed in Caffenol, a coffee-based alternative to traditional developers like Rodinal.
